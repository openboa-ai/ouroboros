# MLP-01 Success Metrics And Launch Bar

## Purpose

This page defines how MLP-01 should be judged.

It does not exist to invent a dashboard.

It exists to define:

- when MLP-01 is launchable
- when MLP-01 is actually successful
- when MLP-01 only looks successful
- when the wedge should be killed or reframed

## Judgment Thesis

The primary judgment question is:

**has the product created one believable delegated live path?**

The secondary judgment question is:

**does that path lead to meaningful live trading behavior rather than ceremonial live presence?**

That means MLP-01 should be judged through proof bars first and light metrics second.

The point of this page is not to maximize activity.

The point is to distinguish:

- believable launch
- real product success
- fake success
- failed product thesis

## Dual North-Star

MLP-01 has two north-stars.

### Primary North-Star: Trustworthy Delegation

The primary north-star is:

**one agent-originated path reaches live in a way that the operator can trust, explain, and
delegate without becoming the permanent runtime loop.**

This means:

- one path becomes real
- one path earns legible counted evidence
- one live approval has clear meaning
- the operator can explain what counted, what was approved, and why intervention happened

### Secondary North-Star: Credible Live Trading Outcome

The secondary north-star is:

**the delegated live path produces meaningful live trading behavior rather than merely existing as a
technical live artifact.**

This means:

- the path is not only running
- the path is not only paper-complete with a live wrapper
- the live outcome is meaningful enough that the path counts as a real trading proof

This is intentionally not a detailed KPI page.

The page is about product judgment, not optimization dashboards.

## Launch Bar

MLP-01 is launch-ready only when the product has crossed a believable trust bar.

Launch-ready does **not** mean "all the main features exist."

It means the product is believable enough to expose as one coherent operator system.

MLP-01 is not launch-ready until all of the following are true:

- one serious path can become one durable candidate
- counted versus non-counted evidence is legible
- one live gate exists with clear meaning
- one delegated live path can run on Binance BTC perpetual futures within explicit limits
- wake and intervention are strong enough that delegation feels believable
- the operator can explain the path from surfaced hypothesis to live operation without relying on
  private implementation knowledge

If these are not true, the product may be technically assembled but is not yet truly launchable.

## Success Bar

MLP-01 should be judged across three distinct bars.

### Technical Success

Technical success means:

- the path works mechanically end to end
- candidate, evaluation, live gate, live execution, and intervention all function
- routine live behavior does not require per-action operator approval

### Product Success

Product success means:

- the operator prefers this path over the stitched manual workflow for the same job
- the product feels like one coherent system rather than disconnected tools
- the trust chain from surfaced path to intervention remains explainable

### Lovable Success

Lovable success means:

- the operator actually feels safe delegating without becoming the runtime again
- the product reduces burden instead of relocating it
- bounded autonomy feels like relief rather than disguised risk transfer

MLP-01 is only genuinely successful when all three bars are true.

## Leading Signals

These are the earliest signals that MLP-01 is progressing toward the lovable proof.

### Trust-proof leading signals

- one surfaced path becomes one durable candidate cleanly
- counted versus non-counted evidence is easy to distinguish
- one live deployment decision has explicit meaning

### Delegation leading signals

- the operator can explain why evidence counted
- the operator can explain what was approved for live
- the operator can explain why the system woke them

### Live-behavior leading signals

- normal live actions happen without per-action human participation
- intervention is reserved for meaningful situations

## Lagging Signals

These are later signals that the wedge is actually working as a product.

- one credible live path exists end to end
- the operator prefers the product over the stitched manual workflow for that path
- intervention is exception-driven rather than constant
- trust survives beyond novelty
- the live path is meaningful enough that it no longer feels like a demo
- the product reduces decision fatigue rather than relocating it

## Evidence Required Before Claiming Success

The team should not claim product success without evidence in all of these categories.

### Proof of path reality

- one surfaced path became one durable candidate
- the operator could follow that path without becoming the system of record

### Proof of legitimacy

- counted versus non-counted evidence was visible
- the live gate was based on a legible rationale

### Proof of delegation

- one promoted path actually ran live
- normal live behavior did not require continuous human shadowing

### Proof of control

- the operator could explain what happened and why
- intervention worked when needed without collapsing back into permanent manual runtime

### Proof of live meaning

- the live path produced meaningful trading behavior
- the team is not mistaking mere live presence for meaningful live outcome

## Fake Success

The clearest fake-success pattern is hidden human labor.

The following do **not** count as real success:

- live appears autonomous but hidden operator labor is still carrying the path
- the path reaches live only because serious judgment still happens outside the product
- the system looks alive, but the operator is still shadow-monitoring continuously
- many surfaced paths exist, but none becomes one believable delegated live path
- live execution works technically, but the operator cannot explain why it was allowed
- product breadth expands while the core lovable proof remains weak

If success requires the operator to secretly remain the runtime, it is fake success.

## Kill Or Reframe Criteria

MLP-01 should be killed or reframed if any of the following become clear:

- the chosen operator does not actually value trustworthy delegation enough
- the product keeps collapsing into idea generation plus manual glue
- bounded autonomy remains emotionally unsafe even when technically correct
- the live path cannot become meaningfully credible without broadening scope before one proof lands
- the wedge can only survive through surface expansion while the lovable proof remains weak

These criteria are about product-thesis failure, not just implementation disappointment.

## Judgment Rule

The team must always distinguish:

- **it works technically**
- **it works as a product**
- **it feels lovable to the chosen operator**

MLP-01 counts only when all three are true.

If only the first is true, the system is assembled.

If the first two are true but not the third, the product may be useful but the lovable proof has
not landed yet.
