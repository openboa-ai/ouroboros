## Goal

Define the evidence ladder required to determine whether CandidateArena's adaptive research loop
actually improves prospective qualified trading-system discovery, whether that effect generalizes,
and which AAR-inspired components cause it.

This program must prevent one successful same-baseline experiment from being relabeled as market
generalization, agent superiority, durable profitability, or production readiness.

## Unbiased Use Of Anthropic AAR

Anthropic's Automated Weak-to-Strong Researcher is relevant design evidence for its studied setting:
long-running independent researchers can produce useful candidate artifacts; broad directed lanes,
shared research memory, external evaluation, and repeated search can matter; and fixed query or
tool caps do not by themselves eliminate evaluator gaming.

It does not establish any of the following for Ouroboros:

- that generated TradingSystems have positive prospective `revenue - cost`;
- that adaptive allocation beats an equal-budget static control;
- that direction assignment, memory, or a managed agent is the causal component;
- that one market window generalizes to another market regime;
- that paper fills approximate production execution closely enough for live authority;
- that an evaluator remains secure after repeated optimization pressure;
- that autonomous scheduling, policy replacement, TradingPromotion, or live execution is safe.

Ouroboros therefore treats AAR as a hypothesis generator and protocol warning, not transferred
proof. Every product claim below needs repository-owned prospective evidence.

## Evidence Ladder

| Level | Claim | Required evidence | Authority after success |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 0. Mechanics | The committed graph executes and recovers correctly. | Deterministic domain, store, runtime, restart, and conformance tests. | Research mechanics only. |
| 1. Same-baseline effect | Adaptive allocation improves qualified discovery under one frozen condition. | One fully precommitted `ResearchControlStudy` with every campaign and slot terminal. | Eligibility for a separate research-policy decision only. |
| 2. Condition generalization | The effect survives precommitted baseline and market-condition blocks. | Multiple independent studies under a stratified protocol with no outcome-aware inclusion. | Evidence to review a broader research policy, not automatic replacement. |
| 3. Component causality | Direction, memory, or agent factors contribute under equal budgets. | Separately precommitted balanced factorial studies with interaction analysis. | Research design guidance only. |
| 4. Economic durability | Selected systems retain qualified net value over longer non-overlapping paper windows. | Continuous paper evidence across time, cost, fill, runner-health, and risk conditions. | Trading review evidence only. |
| 5. Deployment readiness | Process ownership and evaluator security survive deployed operation. | Listener-capable soak, cross-process lease, failure injection, evaluator audit, and operator controls. | Still no private/live authority without a separate explicit product decision. |

Passing one level does not imply the next.

## Gate 2: Baseline And Market-Condition Generalization

Generalization requires a new protocol, not extra fields on one same-baseline study.

### Pre-Effect Blocking

Before any included research effect, commit:

- exact study IDs and baseline snapshot digests;
- a public-market condition-classification algorithm and digest;
- its lookback, timestamp boundary, source configuration, and missing-data rule;
- the required condition blocks and studies per block;
- per-study replication counts and total maximum resource budget;
- one stratified analysis policy and stopping rule.

Condition labels must be derived only from public evidence available before the corresponding study.
Post-outcome labeling, dropping an inconvenient block, extending only favorable blocks, or changing
the classifier after seeing qualified-discovery results invalidates the program.

The first implementation should use coarse, externally reproducible feature bins rather than an
LLM-judged market narrative. Candidate examples are volatility, directional persistence, funding
stress, and public-liquidity/cost conditions, but exact bins and thresholds require a separately
versioned protocol.

### Unit Of Inference

Campaign pairs remain the within-study observations. Baseline/condition blocks are the units of
generalization. Replications from one baseline must not be treated as independent market regimes.

The stratified analysis must:

- weight precommitted blocks explicitly so an oversampled block cannot dominate by accident;
- report every block's adaptive-minus-static effect and eligibility status;
- preserve ties, missed windows, and ineligible slots in declared denominators;
- report heterogeneity and any harmful block, not only a pooled mean;
- use a precommitted exact or independently reproducible sign-flip procedure;
- state `insufficient_generalization_evidence` when required blocks or non-tied counts are absent.

No broad policy decision is eligible when a required block is missing, evidence is materially
harmful in a predeclared block, or the aggregate result depends on a post-hoc weighting choice.

## Gate 3: Component Factorials

AAR-inspired components must be tested as interventions rather than inferred from correlation.
Do not start with one large `2^N` experiment. Run bounded programs in this order after Gate 2:

1. **Direction assignment:** preassigned broad research lanes versus an equal-budget undirected
   researcher that chooses its own lane before development evidence.
2. **Research memory:** bounded prior Finding/Lineage/notebook context versus a context-cleared arm;
   both receive the same current candidate, tools, model, and budgets.
3. **Researcher implementation:** a managed agent versus a specified non-agent search baseline with
   the same candidate contract, evaluation access, wall-clock cap, and compute/tool budget.

Each program must precommit balanced assignment within baseline/condition blocks, primary and
interaction estimands, sample size, exclusion rules, and maximum cost. The existing adaptive/static
allocation factor should remain fixed while testing another component unless the protocol is
explicitly powered for their interaction.

The non-agent baseline cannot be a no-op fixture. It must be a credible reproducible search process;
otherwise the result measures comparator weakness rather than agent contribution.

## Evaluation Independence And Reward-Hacking Pressure

All gates preserve the same authority split:

- researchers generate candidate code and development hypotheses;
- external evaluators own sealed conformance and prospective paper evidence;
- TradingSystems never grade themselves or choose inclusion;
- reserved slots and failed attempts stay in the denominator;
- raw sealed scenarios, evaluator outcomes, and private/live surfaces remain hidden;
- suite/configuration changes create a new evidence cohort rather than a compatibility alias;
- evaluator anomalies, repeated probing, and protocol failures are reported alongside performance;
- longer use requires rotated holdouts or genuinely new future windows, not repeated optimization on
  one fixed exposed benchmark.

Budget caps are containment, not proof against reward hacking. A candidate that exploits the paper
protocol is evidence about evaluator weakness, not a qualified trading discovery.

## Success And Stop Rules

### Continue

- Gate 1 completes exactly and supports or rejects the same-baseline effect.
- Negative and null outcomes remain reproducible and inform redesign.
- Gate 2 begins only after the real arm/session path has listener-capable evidence.
- Gate 3 begins only after condition blocks and baseline independence are operationally credible.

### Redesign

- non-tied samples remain insufficient at the precommitted maximum;
- most slots expire or fail infrastructure rather than produce evaluable evidence;
- evaluator drift or protocol gaming dominates candidate outcomes;
- block heterogeneity makes one global allocation policy misleading;
- the credible baseline cannot be run under equal resources.

### Stop

- exact graph recovery cannot preserve planned denominators;
- private/live authority leaks into research execution;
- repeated studies show no practical qualified-discovery lift at the declared cost;
- apparent gains disappear under fresh baseline/condition blocks;
- operational cost exceeds the bounded research value even when discovery improves.

Stopping preserves all negative findings and lineage. It does not select static control by default;
it leaves the broader adaptive claim unsupported.

## Verification Targets

### Mechanics

- every commitment predates its effects;
- IDs, digests, conditions, blocks, and assignments replay exactly;
- restart executes only the earliest missing action;
- one process owns one active study and one arm owns one runtime activation coordinator;
- unowned sessions are recovered, never adopted;
- waiting uses exact persisted wake times without polling;
- every terminal path is externally classifiable and authority-closed.

### Prospective Evidence

- one listener-capable same-baseline study reaches exact terminal closure;
- all planned campaign and slot denominators reconcile independently;
- paper market/fill, runner health, cost, Ledger, lineage, and failure evidence reload after restart;
- the exact study inference can be recomputed from immutable source records;
- a policy decision, when requested, binds only the exact eligible study and current policy digest.

### Generalization And Factorials

- block assignment uses only pre-effect public evidence;
- required blocks cannot be omitted after outcomes;
- pooled and per-block effects reconcile under the frozen analysis;
- balanced factorial assignments and interactions are reconstructable;
- component claims remain narrower than economic or deployment claims.

## Non-Goals

- Treating AAR results as trading evidence.
- Retrofitting current same-baseline records with a broader causal label.
- Post-hoc regime labeling or outcome-aware sample extension.
- One giant factorial before credible baseline and session operation.
- Automatic allocation-policy replacement, TradingPromotion, private reads, credentials, orders,
  or live execution.
